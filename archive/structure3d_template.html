The model’s representations of one labeled 4×5 lattice, in 3D

Two views per topology, both computed from the trained bilinear attention-only model on one fixed token labeling. behavior eigenmap: spectral embedding of the model’s implied transition matrix — the unfolded lattice. residual PCA: Park et al.-style top principal components of the windowed per-token mean residual stream — our small models organize this as an anti-map (neighboring nodes pushed apart, so the true edges, in gray, cross the middle). Use the context slider to watch the anti-organization form as the walk reveals the graph. Drag to rotate, scroll to zoom; x/y/z menus pick which embedding dimensions you view.

What to look for: in behavior mode the grid unfolds into a sheet, the cylinder into a tube (dashed wrap edges close one direction), and the torus needs all four dims (e1–e2 and e3–e4 are its two circles). In residual PCA mode the same edges stretch across the cloud instead of staying local — the opposite of what Park et al. found in LLMs, where the top PCs draw the lattice itself. The cycle panel is the original cycle-task model: there the residual stream does hold the structure directly — its phase centroids form the ring in the top PCs.
