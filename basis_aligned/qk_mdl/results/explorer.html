Token tables & two heads

Nearly everything this 546M transformer's attention does with long-range context can be replaced by lookup tables indexed by token identity — no training, +0.059 nats. What can't be tabled fits in one sentence: an induction head that matches, a rank-one head that gates, and the top MLPs' fast structural state.

1The wall: making selection token-static the hard way

Layer 0 folds exactly: attention there depends only on the two token identities and their distance, so its maps become vocabulary-indexed tables and the patched model reproduces the live one to ~10⁻¹⁵. Per layer, the same idea works shockingly well even above layer 0. But compose it across all 17 layers and every route hits a wall — including 15.3M jointly CE-trained table parameters. The controls show why the obvious fixes fail: re-estimating each layer's tables under the already-patched stack (fresh statistics, zero training) is even worse, so the wall was never stale statistics.

Routes to fully token-static selection, 546M model. Grey = untrained score-space routes; steel = trained; blue = the windowed architecture (§3), untrained. Dashed line: the +0.114 floor from the four deleted layers alone.

2What selection actually reads

Definitions first. The input to layer L is exactly a sum of streams: the embedding path plus every lower layer's attention output and MLP output — at layer 5 that's 11 tensors. Because the maps into q and k are linear and both norms are per-position scalars, every attention score splits exactly into a sum over stream pairs:

xᵢ = Σₖ sₖ(i) (exact — gate-checked) S(i,j) = q(xᵢ)·k(xⱼ)/h = Σₖₒ q(sₖ(i))·k(sₒ(j))/h energy(a,b) = mean over sampled causal (i,j) of [ q(sₖ(i))·k(sₒ(j))/h ]²

Each (a,b) term reads: "stream a on the query side interacting with stream b on the key side." The chart plots, per layer, the share of total energy in pairs where both streams were created ≤2 layers back ("recent"). The same decomposition applies to the bilinear-MLP hidden — the 4608-dim vector Wₗx̂ ⊙ Wᵣx̂ before the down-projection splits over stream pairs the same way (weighted by down-column norms); it was measured at five layers only (2, 5, 13, 16, 17), hence the sparse square markers.

Bottom layers read a short window (L2: 99%); the middle goes diffuse — with layer 5's attention output persisting as a global "hub" stream in the top pairs everywhere — and the top re-concentrates. Embedding×embedding energy is ≈0 above layer 1: the embedding's selection role is entirely mediated through MLP-0.

Then the causal test. The chart above is observational — where the energy sits. The intervention replaces streams in the QK read (q,k inputs only; values and the residual stay live) with token-indexed conditional-mean tables and measures ΔCE:

Patch only layer L's QK read, three ways. At L5 — the one layer whose selection genuinely uses context — tabling everything costs +0.231, but tabling only the streams older than 2 layers (the ones carrying 11% of its energy above) costs +0.003. The energy map predicted which intervention would be free; the intervention confirmed it. Deep context tables away everywhere — the wall was never "selection needs context," it was "context must not travel live through 17 layers."

3The crack: windowed code propagation

So build exactly that: at every layer, each read of the residual (selection, content, MLP input) sees the exact embedding path, plus conditional-mean tables for streams older than W layers, plus the live streams inside the window. Error chains are bounded at depth W instead of 17. No training anywhere:

bilin18, QK reads bilin18, qk+v+MLP(L1–12) sqrd12, QK reads sqrd12, ALL reads
ΔCE (log scale) vs live-window size W, all points untrained. Horizontal lines: the trained walls from §1. Cost roughly halves per +1 of W. Bonus rows: vq1024 on the tables is free (≈50× table compression, it even denoises slightly), and CE-polishing the table values afterwards buys nothing — the discrete structure, not the values, carries the description. Note the inversion: sqrd12 was 15× harder than bilin18 under score-space tables and is easier here. Compressibility belongs to the (model, decomposition-family) pair.

6The two heads that resist everything

Across 162 head-instances, exactly two carry irreducibly contextual selection — both in layer 5, and they split the program's oldest dichotomy between them: one selects, one carries.

Layer 5, per head: cost of tabling the head alone (x) vs cost of zero-ablating it on repeated-sequence prediction (y, log scale). Seven heads cluster at the origin — tables are free and they barely matter. H5 and H7 separate, in opposite directions: H5 has the induction signature, H7 has the causal weight.

L5.H5 — the match head

Textbook induction pattern: attention on "key follows my previous occurrence" is 16.8× baseline on natural text, 53× on repeats, positional profile flat to Δ64. Its carried content decodes to the source token (logit-lens median rank 25/50k) — identity carriage.

But the identity is carried noisily: cleaning it to conditional means improves repeat CE by −0.17; low-rank filtering by −0.33; amplifying the head ×4 costs +3.37. The model rationally under-weights its own induction signal because the signal is noisy.

L5.H7 — the gain head

No match structure in any context; strictly local profile. Yet zero-ablating it costs +1.04 natural, +6.68 on repeats — the causal heavy lifter, feeding the hub stream the whole upper model reads. Its mean content decodes to the same connectives for every source token:

- and (, in or

Deviations: ~5% of output energy, 63% in ONE direction. Replacing the head by that fixed direction × a live scalar costs +0.0001. H7 is one number per position.

7The top MLPs: diffuse input, a few live gains — and a trap

The one component windowing can't absorb. Input side: broad aggregation (layer 13 draws only 19% of its bilinear energy from recent streams; no dominant channel). Output side: low-rank — mlp16 is a token mean plus ~4–16 contextual directions with live gains. The directions look like document-register features:

The trap (and the causal test that caught it): these look like slow document-register state — but replacing the top-4 coefficients with their document averages costs +0.103, nearly as bad as deleting them (+0.113), against +0.023 live. The directions fire in register-specific contexts while carrying fast, token-to-token structural position within those registers. Lens + examples name where a feature lives; only intervention says what it carries.

8What didn't work (and why that's load-bearing)

The honest half of the story, kept deliberately:

  • Backward metrics (method E): null. Fisher-weighted, unembedding-projected, and directly behavioral clustering objectives all converge into the same basin as plain L2 — because table-quantization error behaves as noise the downstream stack filters. Bonus discovery: the "seed floor" (±0.03) isn't even seeds — GPU-atomic reductions make k-means chaotically non-deterministic at fixed seed.
  • Behavioral refinement repairs, doesn't transcend: gradient-guided reassignment walks an unlucky partition (+0.142) to best-tier (+0.103) and stops. Mass moves reliably backfire — first-order scores demand small steps and revert-on-worsen.
  • Marginals never compose (heads, blocks, layers, even deletions: up to 10× superadditive) — with exactly one additive exception, qk+v windowing, where errors are independent filtered noise. Every headline number here is a composed audit.
  • Training repairs co-adaptation, not structure: it took layer-0's grand codebook from +0.455 to −0.019, and did precisely nothing for windowed tables (+0.094→+0.093). Where the discrete structure is right, there is nothing left for gradients to fix.
